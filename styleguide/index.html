This Is a Style Guide (And Headline 1)

How to make a post sexy in three easy steps. First, make sure you can see the advanced editing options by clicking on the "Show/Hide Kitchen Sink" button in the toolbar.

Step 1. Use headlines. (This is Headline 2.)

Rather than trying to delineate content sections with bolded text, use a headline to really create vertical separation.

Headline Tips

  1. Breaking content with a headline every paragraph or two aids legibility
  2. Make headlines tell the story on their own
  3. Keep them punchy and loaded with keywords

However. (This is Headline 3.)

Keep in mind that Heading 1 is used for the site title and Heading 2 is used for the article/page title, so you shouldn't use those in the body text.

    Other Considerations

    Some other things to think about.

    SEO Stuff

    It's probably worth coming up with an SEO title and a meta description for each post. This is what will show up on Facebook when people share, and it's also what Google will show people when they search.

    Featured Image

    Set a featured image at the bottom of the right-hand column. This will be displayed on Facebook when the post is shared.

    Your Post Slug

    The slug (called the permalink above the editing box) helps SEO and linkability. WordPress will automatically generate one for you, but it's worth double-checking before you post.

    IMPORTANT: Don't change permalinks (This is Headline 4)

    DON'T change this after you've published. It will break any links to the post that live anywhere else on the internet.
